There are multiple different ways to play Melvor Idle. In addition to the official game modes that can be selected when creating a character, there are also community-created game modes that add additional self-imposed restrictions to what a given character is allowed to do.

# Official Game Modes

## Standard

This is the default Melvor Idle game mode. As the default way of playing the game, it doesn't have any unique modifiers.

## Hardcore

Main article: [Hardcore](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore")

Hardcore characters are deleted upon [Death](/w/Combat#Death "Combat") and also have several penalties that apply while playing:

- [![](/images/thumb/6/66/Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png/23px-Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") [Hitpoints](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") do not passively regenerate
- The [Bank](/w/Bank "Bank") is limited to a maximum of 108 slots - 20 slots are available initially and a further 88 can be purchased from the [Shop](/w/Shop "Shop"). If the [![](/images/thumb/c/c9/ItA.png/25px-ItA.png)](/w/Into_the_Abyss_Expansion "Into the Abyss Expansion") [Into the Abyss Expansion](/w/Into_the_Abyss_Expansion "Into the Abyss Expansion") is owned, an additional 88 bank slot can be purchased for 5,000,000[![AP](/images/2/21/Abyssal_Pieces.svg)](/w/Abyssal_Pieces "Abyssal Pieces") AP each for a total of 196 purchase-able bank slots. Players can gain additional slots to exceed this limit, either permanently through the use of [![](/images/thumb/2/2d/Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png/23px-Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png)](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token") [Bank Slot Tokens](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token"), [![](/images/e/e2/TotH.svg)](/w/Throne_of_the_Herald_Expansion "Throne of the Herald Expansion") [![](/images/thumb/2/26/Rosaniya_%28constellation%29.png/23px-Rosaniya_%28constellation%29.png)](/w/Rosaniya "Rosaniya") [Rosaniya](/w/Rosaniya "Rosaniya"), or temporarily while the [![](/images/thumb/e/e5/Agility_%28skill%29.png/23px-Agility_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Agility#PillarofGenerosity "Agility") [Pillar of Generosity](/w/Agility#PillarofGenerosity "Agility") is active.
- The [Combat Triangle](/w/Combat_Triangle "Combat Triangle") penalties for using the combat style that is weak against the current enemy are increased.

## Adventure

Main article: [Adventure](/w/Adventure "Adventure")

**Adventure** mode is a Game Mode in which players start with only a few skills and must work to unlock others. It includes the following changes:

### Melvor Realm Changes

- Skills are locked and must be purchased using [![](/images/thumb/f/f0/Coins.png/23px-Coins.png)](/w/Coins "Coins") [GP](/w/Coins "Coins") to access
- Start with only [![](/images/thumb/4/4b/Attack_%28skill%29.png/23px-Attack_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Attack "Attack") [Attack](/w/Attack "Attack"), [![](/images/thumb/2/27/Strength_%28skill%29.png/23px-Strength_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Strength "Strength") [Strength](/w/Strength "Strength"), [![](/images/thumb/4/4d/Defence_%28skill%29.png/23px-Defence_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Defence "Defence") [Defence](/w/Defence "Defence"), and [![](/images/thumb/6/66/Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png/23px-Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") [Hitpoints](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") unlocked along with 10 [![](/images/thumb/5/5a/Shrimp_%28item%29.png/23px-Shrimp_%28item%29.png)](/w/Shrimp "Shrimp") [Shrimp](/w/Shrimp "Shrimp") and a [![](/images/thumb/0/0b/Bronze_Sword_%28item%29.png/23px-Bronze_Sword_%28item%29.png)](/w/Bronze_Sword "Bronze Sword") [Bronze Sword](/w/Bronze_Sword "Bronze Sword").
- Non-Combat skill levels cannot go above your [Combat Level](/w/Combat_Level "Combat Level").
  - When a skill level reaches its limit, [experience](/w/Experience_Table "Experience Table") can be gained up to the experience required for the next level minus 1 experience point.
  - With a Combat Level at 99 ([![](/images/e/e2/TotH.svg)](/w/Throne_of_the_Herald_Expansion "Throne of the Herald Expansion") 120) or higher, experience in skills is still limited to experience required for the next virtual level minus 1 experience point (e.g., with a combat level at 99, skill experience is limited to 14,391,159).
- The [Combat Triangle](/w/Combat_Triangle "Combat Triangle") penalties for using a combat style the enemy is resistant to are increased (identical to the changes in [![](/images/thumb/1/17/Hardcore.png/23px-Hardcore.png)](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") [Hardcore](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") mode)
- Hitpoints, Damage, and [Food](/w/Food "Food") Healing values are 10x larger than in Standard or Hardcore modes. This is purely a cosmetic change - all gains that are based on HP work as in other modes.

When the lowest level of these skills reaches a number that's divisible by 10 (10, 20, 30, etc) then the player will be able to raise the abyssal level of skills by 10.   - Each skill has its own abyssal level cap increase that must be purchased and each purchase will raise the cost of future increases. The [Level Cap costs](#Abyssal_Level_Cap_Costs) will cap at 1 billion [![AP](/images/2/21/Abyssal_Pieces.svg)](/w/Abyssal_Pieces "Abyssal Pieces") at the 60th abyssal level cap increase.
  - For [![Prayer](/images/thumb/1/11/Prayer_%28skill%29.png/23px-Prayer_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Prayer "Prayer")[Prayer](/w/Prayer "Prayer"), [![Slayer](/images/thumb/8/87/Slayer_%28skill%29.png/23px-Slayer_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Slayer "Slayer")[Slayer](/w/Slayer "Slayer"), and [![Corruption](/images/thumb/3/3d/Corruption_%28skill%29.png/23px-Corruption_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Corruption "Corruption")[Corruption](/w/Corruption "Corruption"), the abyssal level cap button is found on the combat screen under "Toggle Skill Progress".

## Ancient Relics

Main article: [Ancient Relics](/w/Ancient_Relics "Ancient Relics")

**Ancient Relics** mode is a Game Mode in which players start with a maximum Skill Level cap of Level 10 and must defeat [Dungeons](/w/Dungeons "Dungeons") to increase it. Any experience earned beyond the skill cap -1 experience from the next level will be deleted.

This game mode can be started on a new character save slot from the character selection screen.

Ancient relics can be located in all Skills to gain unique permanent modifiers. There are 5 Relics that can be found for each skill. Upon locating all 5 Relics, you will immediately be awarded with the Master Relic. A random guaranteed Melvor Realm Relic is awarded when the respective skill reaches level 20 and level 99, given there are still relics to locate for that skill. Similarly, Abyssal Realm Relics are awarded at Abyssal level 10 and Abyssal level 60.

### Acquiring Ancient Relics

Ancient Relics can be obtained whenever perform an action in a skill, even if the experience goes into the void due to level caps. The drop rate of relics depends on the type of Skill and how many relics the player already has. This is because when rolling for a relic, every relic that has not been found (for that skill) will get a roll.

Ancient Relics are rolled for with each action so decreasing action time will increase your chances over time. Melvor Realm combat relics can only be obtained while fighting in [Combat Areas](/w/Combat_Areas "Combat Areas"), [Slayer Areas](/w/Slayer_Areas "Slayer Areas"), [Dungeons](/w/Dungeons "Dungeons") and [Strongholds](/w/Strongholds "Strongholds"). Abyssal Realm combat relics can only be obtained in [Abyssal Combat Areas](/w/Combat_Areas#Abyssal_Realm "Combat Areas"), [Abyssal Slayer Areas](/w/Slayer_Areas#Abyssal_Realm "Slayer Areas"), [The Abyss](/w/The_Abyss "The Abyss") and [Abyssal Strongholds](/w/Strongholds "Strongholds"). [![](/images/thumb/1/11/Prayer_%28skill%29.png/23px-Prayer_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Prayer "Prayer") [Prayer](/w/Prayer "Prayer") relics are only rolled while using prayers and [![](/images/thumb/8/87/Slayer_%28skill%29.png/23px-Slayer_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Slayer "Slayer") [Slayer](/w/Slayer "Slayer") relics are only rolled while on a slayer task.

# Temporary Modes

The following game modes were introduced on a temporary basis in the past, and may no longer be available to play.

## Chaos

Main article: [Chaos](/w/Chaos "Chaos")

[![](/images/thumb/8/8f/Chaos.png/23px-Chaos.png)](/w/Chaos "Chaos") [Chaos](/w/Chaos "Chaos") mode was a game mode released as part of the [2021 April Fools Event](/w/Events#April_Fools_2021 "Events"). It was a time limited event: new characters could only be made in Chaos Mode until April 11th, 2021, and the game mode was discontinued as part of the [Alpha v0.21](/w/V0.21 "V0.21") update. It included the following features:

- Item Corruption
- 25% interval reduction on attack speeds & skill intervals (multiplicative)
- All enemies have at least 1 random passive
- All equipped items are lost on death
- BIG NUMBERS
- [Golbin Raid](/w/Golbin_Raid "Golbin Raid") is not available
- The [Combat Triangle](/w/Combat_Triangle "Combat Triangle") penalties for using a combat style the enemy is resistant to are increased. (Identical to the changes in Hardcore mode)
- Do not fight a [![](/images/thumb/6/60/Chicken_%28monster%29.png/23px-Chicken_%28monster%29.png)](/w/Chicken_(monster) "Chicken (monster)") [Chicken](/w/Chicken_(monster) "Chicken (monster)")

## Hardcore Adventure Speedrun

Main article: [Hardcore Adventure Speedrun](/w/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun "Hardcore Adventure Speedrun")

The [![](/images/thumb/9/93/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun.png/23px-Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun.png)](/w/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun "Hardcore Adventure Speedrun") [Hardcore Adventure Speedrun](/w/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun "Hardcore Adventure Speedrun") Game Mode was introduced as part of the [April Fools Event 2022](/w/April_Fools_Event_2022 "April Fools Event 2022"). It was a limited time game mode: Characters could be created for this game mode from April 1st, 2022; and could be played until April, 30th, 2022.

The aim of the game mode was to defeat [![](/images/thumb/9/97/Bane_%28monster%29.png/23px-Bane_%28monster%29.png)](/w/Bane "Bane") [Bane](/w/Bane "Bane") in the fastest time possible, with the following changes from the standard game mode:

- Hardcore rules apply
- Adventure rules apply

- Double [![](/images/thumb/6/66/Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png/23px-Hitpoints_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") [Maximum Hitpoints](/w/Hitpoints "Hitpoints") for you and the enemies.
- -80% [Attack Interval](/w/Attack_Interval "Attack Interval") for you and the enemies.
- -80% Skill intervals
- +800% Global mastery XP
- -2s Monster Respawn

- Stability not guaranteed

## Internal Suffering Speedrun

Main article: [Internal Suffering Speedrun](/w/Internal_Suffering_Speedrun "Internal Suffering Speedrun")

The [![](/images/thumb/e/ea/Internal_Suffering_Speedrun.png/23px-Internal_Suffering_Speedrun.png)](/w/Internal_Suffering_Speedrun "Internal Suffering Speedrun") [Internal Suffering Speedrun](/w/Internal_Suffering_Speedrun "Internal Suffering Speedrun") Game Mode was introduced during [V1.0.5](/w/V1.0.5 "V1.0.5"). It is a limited time game mode which was available to play until August 31 23:59:59 GMT.

The aim of the game mode is to defeat [![](/images/thumb/9/97/Bane_%28monster%29.png/23px-Bane_%28monster%29.png)](/w/Bane "Bane") [Bane](/w/Bane "Bane") in the fastest time possible, although the mode has some unique mechanics which complicate this:

- The game mode is safe - Characters are not lost upon death.
- All skills except [![](/images/thumb/b/b5/Firemaking_%28skill%29.png/23px-Firemaking_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking") [Firemaking](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking") and [![](/images/thumb/8/88/Farming_%28skill%29.png/23px-Farming_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Farming "Farming") [Farming](/w/Farming "Farming") are available from the start.
- The player's skill level for all skills is initially capped at level 10.
- This cap can be increased by 10 each time the player chooses to permanently disable any non-combat skill, so the player will be able to reach level 99 once 9 skills have been disabled. Once disabled, a skill may no longer be used.
- Uses the same [Combat Triangle](/w/Combat_Triangle "Combat Triangle") as [![](/images/thumb/1/17/Hardcore.png/23px-Hardcore.png)](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") [Hardcore](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") mode, except it is reversed - [![](/images/thumb/4/4b/Attack_%28skill%29.png/23px-Attack_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Melee "Melee") [Melee](/w/Melee "Melee") beats [![](/images/thumb/2/24/Magic_%28skill%29.png/23px-Magic_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Magic "Magic") [Magic](/w/Magic "Magic"), [![](/images/thumb/0/0c/Ranged_%28skill%29.png/23px-Ranged_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Ranged "Ranged") [Ranged](/w/Ranged "Ranged") beats [![](/images/thumb/4/4b/Attack_%28skill%29.png/23px-Attack_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Melee "Melee") [Melee](/w/Melee "Melee"), and [![](/images/thumb/2/24/Magic_%28skill%29.png/23px-Magic_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Magic "Magic") [Magic](/w/Magic "Magic") beats [![](/images/thumb/0/0c/Ranged_%28skill%29.png/23px-Ranged_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Ranged "Ranged") [Ranged](/w/Ranged "Ranged").

This game mode also has modifiers similar to [![](/images/thumb/9/93/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun.png/23px-Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun.png)](/w/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun "Hardcore Adventure Speedrun") [Hardcore Adventure Speedrun](/w/Hardcore_Adventure_Speedrun "Hardcore Adventure Speedrun"), significantly increasing the rate of progression:

- -80% [Attack Interval](/w/Attack_Interval "Attack Interval") for both the player and [Monsters](/w/Monsters "Monsters").
- -80% Skill intervals
- +800% Global [![](/images/thumb/5/58/Mastery.png/23px-Mastery.png)](/w/Mastery "Mastery") [Mastery](/w/Mastery "Mastery") XP
- -2s Monster respawn timer

# Community Created Variations

The following game modes aren't enforced in-game but instead have a list of rules that players who wish to play them choose to follow voluntarily. Different players may play the same game mode slightly differently or may choose to combine multiple to create an even more difficult challenge.

Some people also combine these game modes, for increased difficulty.

## Game Modes

These modes are mostly collections of restrictions, or unique ways to play the game which are widespread enough to be talked about to some degree or another in an agreed-upon way.

### Hardcore Combat Only (HCCO)

Main article: [Hardcore Combat Only](/w/Hardcore_Combat_Only "Hardcore Combat Only")

Hardcore Combat Only (often abbreviated to HCCO) is a game mode in which the player creates a [![](/images/thumb/1/17/Hardcore.png/23px-Hardcore.png)](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") [Hardcore](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") character which completely avoids non-combat skills. All gear and food can only be obtained through combat, so success in this game mode requires careful planning in order to grow stronger without dying, especially due to the lack of passive HP regeneration.

### PainCO

Main article: [PainCO](/w/PainCO "PainCO")

This is pain: A harder start to HCCO, with additional limitations such as some restricted monsters, being unable to use the shop, and no upgrading of items.

### Masochism

Main article: [Masochism](/w/Masochism "Masochism")

This mode is not meant to be easy, nor is it meant to be quick. It is meant so be slow, tedious, and to punish mistakes. Played in [![](/images/thumb/f/ff/Adventure.png/23px-Adventure.png)](/w/Adventure "Adventure") [Adventure](/w/Adventure "Adventure") it is designed to make players think heavily about preparation and planning. In addition there are strong bank management aspects involved that can very easily punish mistakes.

## Restrictions

### 12 Bank Slots (12B)

12 Bank Slots (often abbreviated as 12B) is a game mode in which players restrict themselves to the starting 12 bank slots and do not purchase more or use [![](/images/thumb/2/2d/Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png/23px-Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png)](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token") [Bank Slot Tokens](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token"). Although often combined with Hardcore Combat Only, this game mode can also be played as a regular character.

This game mode can be compared to the UIM challenge on [RuneScape](https://www.runescape.com).

### Bank Completion

#### Living Bank

Bank Completion, also called Living Bank, is an extension of Item Completion (getting 100% of items in [Completion Log](/w/Completion_Log "Completion Log")), where at least one of each [item](/w/Table_of_Items "Table of Items") is in the bank. This requires 2,494 [Bank Slots](/w/Bank_Slot "Bank Slot").

Items that do not count for Completion usually are excluded from this challenge since they include [unobtainable](/w/Unlisted_Items "Unlisted Items") items such as the [![](/images/thumb/b/bb/Purple_Party_Hat_%28item%29.png/23px-Purple_Party_Hat_%28item%29.png)](/w/Purple_Party_Hat "Purple Party Hat") [Purple Party Hat](/w/Purple_Party_Hat "Purple Party Hat"), which cannot be acquired after the event, rendering the challenge otherwise impossible.

#### BXNK

BXNK is the next step to Living Bank, where X is an arbitrary number, meaning you require X amount of every item.

The most common values of X are 5, 6, and 9, which are the amount of [![](/images/thumb/5/5d/Extra_Equipment_Set_%28upgrade%29.png/23px-Extra_Equipment_Set_%28upgrade%29.png)](/w/Extra_Equipment_Set "Extra Equipment Set") [equipment sets](/w/Extra_Equipment_Set "Extra Equipment Set") available for each expansion ([![Melvor Logo.svg](/images/0/0c/Melvor_Logo.svg)](/w/Full_Version "Full Version") 4, [![](/images/e/e2/TotH.svg)](/w/Throne_of_the_Herald_Expansion "Throne of the Herald Expansion") 5, and [![](/images/thumb/c/c9/ItA.png/25px-ItA.png)](/w/Into_the_Abyss_Expansion "Into the Abyss Expansion") 8) + 1 for the bank.

The goal is to have enough duplicate [equipment](/w/Equipment "Equipment") to fill all sets, and still have one of each item in the bank. This is called **Ultimate Living Bank**.

While it is possible in [Standard](#Standard) game mode to get enough bank slots, it is difficult in [![](/images/thumb/1/17/Hardcore.png/23px-Hardcore.png)](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") [Hardcore](/w/Hardcore "Hardcore") due to the 108 bank slot limit from the [![](/images/thumb/3/31/Shop.png/23px-Shop.png)](/w/Shop "Shop") [Shop](/w/Shop "Shop"). One would need to use at least 2,386 [![](/images/thumb/2/2d/Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png/23px-Bank_Slot_Token_%28item%29.png)](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token") [Bank Slot Tokens](/w/Bank_Slot_Token "Bank Slot Token") after hitting the 108 slot limit.

### Combat Drops Only

While often considered as a "more relaxed" version of Combat Only, some people play Combat Drops Only/No Gathering (abbreviated CDO, or NG), where they are only allowed to craft items from resources collected from enemies.

The only skills they are allowed to use are [![](/images/thumb/b/b5/Firemaking_%28skill%29.png/23px-Firemaking_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking") [Firemaking](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking"), [![](/images/thumb/8/8f/Cooking_%28skill%29.png/23px-Cooking_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Cooking "Cooking") [Cooking](/w/Cooking "Cooking"), [![](/images/thumb/c/c8/Smithing_%28skill%29.png/23px-Smithing_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Smithing "Smithing") [Smithing](/w/Smithing "Smithing"), [![](/images/thumb/8/88/Farming_%28skill%29.png/23px-Farming_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Farming "Farming") [Farming](/w/Farming "Farming"), [![](/images/thumb/0/03/Fletching_%28skill%29.png/23px-Fletching_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Fletching "Fletching") [Fletching](/w/Fletching "Fletching"), [![](/images/thumb/8/89/Crafting_%28skill%29.png/23px-Crafting_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Crafting "Crafting") [Crafting](/w/Crafting "Crafting"), [![](/images/thumb/8/82/Runecrafting_%28skill%29.png/23px-Runecrafting_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Runecrafting "Runecrafting") [Runecrafting](/w/Runecrafting "Runecrafting"), [![](/images/thumb/a/a9/Herblore_%28skill%29.png/23px-Herblore_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Herblore "Herblore") [Herblore](/w/Herblore "Herblore"), [![](/images/thumb/2/24/Magic_%28skill%29.png/23px-Magic_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Alternative_Magic "Alternative Magic") [Alt. Magic](/w/Alternative_Magic "Alternative Magic"), and those related to combat.

As a note, there is currently no way to level [![](/images/thumb/b/b5/Firemaking_%28skill%29.png/23px-Firemaking_%28skill%29.png)](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking") [Firemaking](/w/Firemaking "Firemaking"), as there is no enemy who drops [![](/images/thumb/1/12/Normal_Logs_%28item%29.png/23px-Normal_Logs_%28item%29.png)](/w/Normal_Logs "Normal Logs") [Normal Logs](/w/Normal_Logs "Normal Logs").

### No-Coward

Disallows fleeing from combat. There's no room for both the enemy and yourself to remain alive! Requires a bit of planning ahead.

### Shopless

Shopless (SL) is as simple it sounds, this game mode doesn't allow the usage of the shop. This limits the user to the initial bank slots until slaying God Dungeons, no skill upgrades, and no [Auto Eat](/w/Auto_Eat "Auto Eat"). Usually, the player may use [![](/images/thumb/f/f0/Coins.png/23px-Coins.png)](/w/Coins "Coins") [Gold Pieces](/w/Coins "Coins") collected to upgrade gear, but there's no purpose to accumulating [![](/images/thumb/7/77/Slayer_Coins.png/23px-Slayer_Coins.png)](/w/Slayer_Coins "Slayer Coins") [Slayer Coins](/w/Slayer_Coins "Slayer Coins").

### Vegan Mode

Vegan players don't like to kill animals nor human beings, but allow themselves to kill monsters (because they're evil, apparently), and plants. Nevertheless, they will still pickpocket the humans, in order to distribute the wealth of the rich.

### Skiller

Also called No Combat (NC): Most normal accounts start out as skiller accounts, and some players decide to stick with it. Players in this game mode never touch combat, and just live a peaceful life gathering resources through hard labour.
